Ceremony 1: Arts & Crafts Ceremony üé®

This ceremony is the school-wide walk. This is the walk for the College of Arts of Sciences (aka College of Arts and Crafts), formally known as the Final Exercises in which I will walk with thousands of my closest friends mostly for kicks and giggles. Doubtful you'll be able to take pictures of me, let alone see me in the mass of people. On the bright side, you will be able to experience a long standing UVA tradition with me that I've been looking forward to for four years üòä

What really happens (from the uva finals website): Each May for Final Exercises, the graduating class, made up of undergraduate and graduate students alike, lines up around the Rotunda for the unforgettable procession down the Lawn. Tens of thousands of parents, guests and faculty members watch as the students make their way down the Lawn. The graduates sit facing away from the Rotunda, toward Old Cabell Hall, to symbolize their entrance into the world beyond the University, taking with them all of their experiences and the knowledge gained during their time on Grounds. Final Exercises begins at 10 a.m. with the procession down the Lawn. The ceremony lasts for approximately two hours.

Time: 10AM (rumor has it you should be there much earlier than that)
Location ‚òÄÔ∏è: the Lawn in front of the Rotunda üèõ Notes: Same deal as Friday, bring clear plastic bags and be prepared to go through metal detectors.
